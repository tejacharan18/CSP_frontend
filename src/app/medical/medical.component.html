• INFLUENZA

    MEDICINES : Oseltamivir,Zanamivir

    TESTS : Rapid Influenza Diagnostic Tests (RIDTs),Reverse Transcription Polymerase Chain Reaction (RT-PCR),Nucleic Acid Amplification Tests (NAATs)

    Zanamivir For the treatment of influenza in adults and children (7 years and older): The recommended dosage is 2 inhalations (5 mg per inhalation) twice daily (approximately 12 hours apart) for a total of 10 doses (20 inhalations) over 5 days.

    Oseltamivir For the treatment of influenza in adults and children (1 year and older): The recommended dosage for most individuals is 75 mg orally twice daily for 5 days.

  • MALARIA

    MEDICINES : Chloroquine,Quinine,Atovaquone-proguanil

    TEST : Microscopic Examination of Blood Smear, Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDTs),Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)

    Chloroquine is an antimalarial drug used for the treatment of uncomplicated malaria caused by Plasmodium vivax, Plasmodium ovale, Plasmodium malariae, and some drug-sensitive strains of Plasmodium falciparum.

    Quinine is an effective treatment for severe malaria and is usually administered in combination with an additional antimalarial drug.

  • TYPHOID

    MEDICINES : Fluoroquinolones, Third-generation Cephalosporins

    TESTS : Blood Culture ,Widal Test, Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)

    Ciprofloxacin and levofloxacin are examples of fluoroquinolone antibiotics that are often used as the first-line treatment for uncomplicated typhoid fever.

    Ceftriaxone and cefixime are examples of third-generation cephalosporin antibiotics that are commonly used as alternative treatments, especially in areas with fluoroquinolone-resistant strains of Salmonella typhi.

  • DIARRHEA

    MEDICINES : Fluoroquinolones, Third-generation Cephalosporins

    TESTS : Stool Ova and Parasite Examination,Stool Antigen Tests,Blood Tests

  • DENGUE

    TESTS : PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction),Dengue IgG Antibody Test

    PCR testing is a molecular diagnostic method used to detect the genetic material (RNA) of the dengue virus. PCR testing is typically conducted in specialized laboratories and is more commonly used for early detection of the virus in suspected cases or in research settings.

  • FEVER

    MEDICINES : Paracetamol (Acetaminophen) ,Aspirin

    For adults: The typical adult dose of paracetamol is 500 mg to 1000 mg every 4 to 6 hours, as needed. The maximum daily dose should not exceed 4000 mg (4 grams).

    For children: The dosage for children is based on their weight. It's important to follow the dosing instructions provided on the specific formulation of paracetamol for children. It's typically administered as a suspension or syrup. Always consult a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage for a child.
